> **Write with the reader in mind**
>
> Everyone **scan reads** … This means you have to **get to the point**.

> **Front-load your writing**
>
> Address audience needs at the top

> **Use you and we**
>
> **You**: not the user, students, staff, the applicant
> **We**: not the department name or organisation name

> **Use active verbs**
>
> … make it clear **who** is expected to do **what**.

> **Concise and scan-friendly**
>
> Structure matters
>
> Cut the padding

> **Use the simplest words that work**
>
> **Need**, not require, requirement
> …
> More examples: A-Z of alternative words from the Plain English Campaign.
>
> Complex words are allowed … Only use **jargon and acronyms** if you're sure the audience will know them.

> **Avoid nominalisations**
>
> Fail, not fail**ure**
> …
> This 5-minute video explains all: Beware of nominalizations (AKA Zombie Nouns) (YouTube)

> **Editing is key**
>
> Edit your own work
>
> Try pair writing for important work
